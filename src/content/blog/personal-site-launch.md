Today I'm launching my new personal site. I've had variations of a personal site in the past. One that was more professional, one that was just a blog. But I never really spent much time on them and they weren't really "living" (my blog only ever had 1 post).

I want this new site to be "my homepage" on the internet. I want it to show a status of what I'm currently working on, share some thoughts, and launch new projects. I love the build in public and indie hacking communities, so that definitely inspires this site for me.

Right now, I'm also extremely excited by agents and coding with agents. I set up OpenClaw about a week ago and it has changed the way I build. The way people write code is changing and the way companies are structured will be changing. But right now, I'm just really excited by it and I'm playing around with it because it's fun and feels right.

This site was completely vibe coded (you can probably tell). I'm going to test the limits of OpenClaw by building everything that has been on my backlog along with some new ideas that I wouldn't have thought of before the modern AI era.
